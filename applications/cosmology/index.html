The Dark Matter Puzzle

Ultra-diffuse galaxies (UDGs) present an extraordinary range of dark matter content that defies simple explanation:

  • Dragonfly 44: 98% dark matter by mass
  • NGC 1052-DF2: Little to no dark matter (old stars!)
  • NGC 1052-DF4: Also dark-matter-deficient despite age
  • AGC 114905: Young, gas-rich, dark-matter-free

Problem for ΛCDM: Galaxies with similar stellar mass should have similar dark matter halos (stellar-to-halo mass relation).

Problem for MOND: DF2 and DF4 follow Newtonian dynamics despite being in low-acceleration regime.

The Temporal Echo Model

We propose dark matter is not primordially assigned but dynamically accumulated—a gravitational wake generated by integrated history.

S(t,x) = ∫₀ᵗ K(t-t'; aₙ(x)) H(t',x) dt' where: - S = wake strength (dimensionless) - K = memory kernel = exp(-τ/τc) - τc = τ₀√(a₀/aₙ) (memory timescale) - H = happening density (interaction rate) - aₙ = Newtonian acceleration

Key Insight: Memory Depends on Acceleration

Low-acceleration regions (aN << a₀) have long memory → accumulate large wake
High-acceleration regions (aN >> a₀) have short memory → wake decays rapidly

Explaining UDG Diversity

The Temporal Echo framework sorts UDGs by integrated history, not primordial halo assignment:

Young Isolated (AGC 114905)

Age: Low
Happening Density: Low (isolated)
DMmetric = Age × H: Very low
Result: Little accumulated wake → dark-matter-free

Old Isolated (Nube)

Age: High (~10 Gyr)
Happening Density: Moderate (past interactions)
DMmetric: High
Result: Significant wake → apparent dark matter

Old Cluster (Dragonfly 44)

Age: High
Happening Density: Very high (cluster environment)
DMmetric: Very high
Result: Massive wake → 98% "dark matter"

Collision Reset (DF2, DF4)

Age: High (~9 Gyr old stars)
But: Recent high-velocity collision!
Mechanism: Baryons mechanically separated from their wake
Result: Old but dark-matter-free

Laminar Wake (Cloud 9) — NEW!

Environment: Stable satellite orbit around M94
Key property: τ_c/t_age ≈ 30 (memory timescale vastly exceeds age)
DM/Baryon ratio: ~5000:1 (extreme!)
Result: Full history integration → maximum wake accumulation
Significance: MOND predicts ~2-40:1, fails by 2+ orders of magnitude

Open Questions & Limitations

⚠ Challenges

  • Galaxy Clusters: TE may underpredict (like MOND). May need residual particle DM or modified H prescription.
  • Gravitational Lensing: Needs full relativistic treatment with stress-energy tensor
  • CMB: How does TE couple to early universe? Structure formation?
  • Solar System: Tidal effects ≲10⁻¹⁶ (safe), but need detailed calculation

✓ Predictions to Test

  • Post-merger galaxies should show reduced DM (wake separated)
  • Tidal dwarfs should be DM-free (new formation, no history)
  • Rotation curve crossover radius rcross ~ √(GMb/a₀) testable
  • Time-variable DM content after major interactions
