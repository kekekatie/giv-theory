The Meson Decay Mystery

Vector mesons span three orders of magnitude in stability:

  • ρ(770): Γ ≈ 150 MeV (broad, unstable)
  • ω(782): Γ ≈ 8.5 MeV
  • φ(1020): Γ ≈ 4.3 MeV
  • J/ψ(3097): Γ ≈ 93 keV (narrow, stable)
  • Υ(9460): Γ ≈ 54 keV (extremely narrow)

Standard QCD attributes this to asymptotic freedom and OZI suppression. But why this specific scaling?
